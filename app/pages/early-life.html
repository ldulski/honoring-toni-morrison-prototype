Early Life

Before she was know as Toni Morrison, she went by a different name…

If there is a book that you want to read, but it hasn’t been written yet, you must be the one to write it.

Toni Morrison

  • Life in Ohio

    Chloe Anthony Wofford, later known as Toni Morrison, was born on February 18, 193. Growing up in Lorian, Ohio in a semi-integrated neighborhood Morrison faced racial discrimination at a young age. When she was baptized, at 12 years old, under the name Saint Anthony of Padua she got the nickname Toni.

  • Education

    Morrison attended Howard University in 1949 earning her bachelors in English. In 1955, she attended Cornell University earning her Master of Arts in English.

  • Career

    Morrison taught English at Southern Texas University but quickly left to start teaching at Howard University for 7 years. Later she moved to Syracuse, New York working as a textbook editor for Random House Publishing. Within 2 years, she was promoted to the New York City office as an editor for fiction books written by African-American authors.

  • Motherhood

    During her time at Howard University she met her husband, Harold Morrison. A few years later she gave birth to her sons Harold and Slade.

Honoring Her Memory

On August 5, 2019, Toni Morrison passed away at a hospital in the Bronx, New York City, at age 88. Her death was due to complications from pneumonia. Morrison is credited with uplifting black voices and stories with her own work and through her time at Random House Publishing.

She helped anthologize the writings of African authors, including Chinua Achebe and Wole Soyinka. Outside of her work as an author and editor, she was a professor inspiring and teaching future generations of authors. Her legacy lives on through her work, colleagues, and her students.
