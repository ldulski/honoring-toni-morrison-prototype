Beloved

Published in 1987 set in America post-Civil War about a family of formerly enslaved people whose home is haunted by a malicious ghost. Won the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction in 1878 and was made into a movie in 1998.

Shop Local

The Bluest Eye

Published in 1970 takes place in Lorain, Ohio. The best-seller tells the story of a young African-American girl named Pecola Breedlove, who grew up following the Great Depression.

Politics & Prose

We've partnered with local DC bookstore Politics & Prose to discover more works by Toni Morrison and other talented authors.

Song of Solomon

Follows the story of Macon “Milkman” Dead III, an African-American man living in Michigan, telling his life story from birth to adulthood. It was published in 1977, earned critical acclaim, and won the National Book Critics Circle Award.

Children's Books

A collection of children’s books written by Toni Morrison and her son Slade Morrison. These books change the narrative of children’s books by including diverse and unique morals.
